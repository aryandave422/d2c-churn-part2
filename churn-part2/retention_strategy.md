## Retention Action per Segment

### 1. Champions
**Who they are:** Frequent, recent, high-spend customers. The top 26% of our base.
**Retention action:**
- Invite to exclusive loyalty program tier (early access to new launches)
- Personalized thank-you note + free product sample
- No discounts needed — reward with experience, not margin erosion
- Ask for product reviews and referrals

**Expected business value:** Retaining Champions prevents the highest revenue loss. One churned Champion ≈ 5 dormant customers in LTV.

---

### 2. Loyal Customers
**Who they are:** Consistent buyers, slightly lower spend than Champions but very reliable.
**Retention action:**
- Upgrade nudge: "You're 2 orders away from Platinum status"
- Personalised product recommendations based on purchase history
- Birthday/anniversary offer with free shipping

**Expected business value:** Moving 10% of Loyal → Champion tier could increase cohort revenue by ~15%.

---

### 3. Potential Loyalists
**Who they are:** Mid-tier in R, F, M — could go either way. Need engagement push.
**Retention action:**
- Educational content (skincare routines, product how-to)
- Offer a loyalty points double-dip event
- Product bundle at slight discount to increase frequency

**Expected business value:** This is the growth segment. Converting 20% to Loyal Customers is realistic with 2-touch engagement.

---

### 4. At-Risk Customers
**Who they are:** Were good customers but recency has spiked (90+ days no order). High churn probability.
**Retention action:**
- "We miss you" personalized email + 15% reactivation coupon (time-limited: 7 days)
- Follow-up SMS on day 4 if no open
- If still no response, move to dormant flow

**Expected business value:** Even 20% reactivation from this segment = ~70 customers × ₹1500 AOV = ₹1,05,000 recovered revenue.

---

### 5. Discount-Sensitive
**Who they are:** Only buy when there's a discount offer. Low margin contribution.
**Retention action:**
- Limit additional discounts — they are already trained on promotions
- Test loyalty-points offers instead of cash discounts
- Introduce minimum cart value for discount eligibility

**Expected business value:** Reducing discount dependency here protects margin without losing the customer entirely.

---

### 6. Dormant Customers
**Who they are:** Haven't ordered in 180+ days, minimal app activity.
**Retention action:**
- One final "Win-Back" email with strong offer (20% off or gift with purchase)
- If no response in 14 days → suppress from active campaigns to reduce cost
- Keep on a quarterly re-engagement drip

**Expected business value:** Win-back rate typically 5-10%. Low ROI but low cost if automated.

---

### 7. New Customers
**Who they are:** First-order customers, recently acquired.
**Retention action:**
- Post-purchase onboarding sequence (day 3, day 7, day 14)
- Product usage tips via email
- Nudge second purchase with "New Member Offer" (free shipping on next order)

**Expected business value:** Second-purchase conversion is the highest ROI moment in a customer lifecycle. Improving 1→2 conversion by 10% compounds heavily.

---

### 8. High Returners
**Who they are:** Return >25% of orders. Likely product-fit or sizing issues.
**Retention action:**
- Trigger a product quiz to improve recommendations
- Proactive CS outreach: "Was there something wrong with your order?"
- Do NOT give automatic discount — investigate root cause first

**Expected business value:** Fixing product-fit for this group reduces return costs and could convert them to loyal buyers.

---

### 9. High-Value But Unhappy
**Who they are:** High RFM score but multiple unresolved support tickets.
**Retention action:**
- IMMEDIATE escalation to senior CS rep
- Personal call from account manager
- Resolve ticket before any marketing contact
- Priority SLA on all future tickets

**Expected business value:** Losing even one customer from this group is a significant LTV loss. Prevention is far cheaper than win-back.

## Campaign Budget Prioritization

**Assumption:** Limited campaign budget of ₹2,00,000 for the next quarter.

| Priority | Segment | Budget Allocation | Rationale |
|----------|---------|------------------|-----------|
| 1 | At-Risk Customers | ₹60,000 (30%) | Highest ROI — reactivating high-value customers |
| 2 | New Customers | ₹40,000 (20%) | 1→2 purchase conversion is critical |
| 3 | Potential Loyalists | ₹35,000 (17.5%) | Growth segment with good conversion probability |
| 4 | High-Value But Unhappy | ₹25,000 (12.5%) | Small group, very high LTV at risk |
| 5 | Dormant Customers | ₹20,000 (10%) | Low win-back rate but automated, low-cost |
| 6 | Champions (experience) | ₹15,000 (7.5%) | Experience rewards, not discounts |
| 7 | High Returners | ₹5,000 (2.5%) | Research/quiz, not campaigns |
| Avoid | Discount-Sensitive | ₹0 | No incremental discount spend this quarter |

**Do NOT** spend on Loyal Customers and Champions with discounts — they will buy anyway. Invest in their experience instead.

---

## Key Non-RFM Signals Used

1. **Unresolved ticket count** — Flags unhappy customers who need CS-first approach
2. **Return rate** — Identifies product-fit issues needing recommendation fix
3. **Days since last session** — Early churn warning before order recency spikes
4. **Campaign conversion rate** — Identifies discount-trained vs genuinely engaged customers
5. **Discount usage rate** — Helps separate discount-driven buyers from value buyers
